About Me

I grew up in a small town in the mountains of southern California. I spend my summers hiking and camping every weekend, and during the winter I snowboard at least once a week. I love to be outdoors and experience new adventures. Most of my free time is spent time either playing or watching sports. Some people even say I have an unnatural love for BYU Football. In recent years I have spent a lot of time traveling. I have been to 10 different countries and love learning about and experiencing new cultures. I had a life-changing opportunity to spend a summer in Madagascar. Ever since I have longed to go back and create a better system than currently exists. I want to use my love for sports to teach children in underdeveloped countries the same skills I learned growing up playing sports. I learned responsibility, respect, and teamwork. I learned how to fail successfully and improve through those failures. Those lessons have shaped me into who I am. I want these children to grow up one day and make lasting changes in their own life and countries. Learn more by checking out my personal branding guide.

Projects

Drift

The DRIFT Lifestyle Boutique at the Riverwoods is centered around offering experiences that help local customers become inspired by nature and connect with the outdoors. We assisted the Riverwoods in building a community of people that have a shared curiosity about the outdoors and create memories that will last forever. Each member of our group took one of five responsibilities for the project: experience vision/direction, merchandising, visual display, marketing, and customer engagement. I focused on marketing.

The TD Experience

The Celtics have been at the heart of Boston for decades. At TD Garden, fans' excitement skyrocket as they watch the game between whistles. As a group we wanted to heighten that experience and help Celtics fans have an exciting experience that includes so much more than just basketball. This new and improved fan experience starts with the venue. We came up with seven ideas that would transform the venue and the experiences for fans.

Swig: Persuading Report

We considered how to inspire change among Swig so they could become more experience-centric in their management practices. We found that an experience-centric approach would create more profit and happier employees. As Swig creates organizational shifts towards agile management and implements these recommendations slowly and effectively, employee and customer experience will significantly improve.

Women Kick Butt
